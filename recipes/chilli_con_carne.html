Chilli Con Carne

The magical chilli con carne

Chilli con carne is a hearty, spicy stew that originated in Texan and Mexican culinary traditions. The dish typically features ground or diced beef simmered with tomatoes, kidney beans, onions, garlic, and a blend of spices like cumin, paprika, and chili powder. It's known for its bold flavor and satisfying texture, making it a favorite comfort food in many households.

While traditional recipes focus on beef, variations now include turkey, pork, or even plant-based alternatives for vegetarian versions. Chilli con carne is often served with rice, cornbread, or tortilla chips, and garnished with sour cream, cheese, or fresh herbs. Its versatility and rich taste have helped it gain popularity around the world.

Ingredients

  • 15 g olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped (about 150 g)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced (about 6 g)
  • 450 g ground beef
  • 2 g ground cumin
  • 2 g chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 g smoked paprika
  • 1 g ground oregano
  • 0.5 g cayenne pepper (optional for extra heat)
  • 400 g chopped tomatoes (1 can)
  • 30 g tomato paste
  • 240 g red kidney beans, drained (1 can)
  • 240 ml beef broth (approx. 240 g)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, shredded cheese, chopped cilantro, sliced jalapeños

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook for 5 minutes, or until softened.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  5. Sprinkle in the cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, oregano, and cayenne pepper. Stir well to coat the meat with the spices.
  6. Add the tomato paste and mix it in thoroughly.
  7. Pour in the chopped tomatoes and beef broth (or add the stock cube with a little hot water). Stir to combine.
  8. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Add the drained kidney beans and continue to simmer for another 10 minutes to heat through and blend flavors.
  10. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Serve hot, topped with your favorite garnishes like sour cream, shredded cheese, or fresh herbs.
