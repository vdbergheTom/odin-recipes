Tomato Basil Soup

Perfect marriage of tomato and basil

Tomato basil soup is a classic, comforting dish that combines the rich acidity of ripe tomatoes with the fresh, aromatic flavor of basil. Often served as a starter, it’s warm, smooth, and deeply satisfying—perfect for cozy evenings or a light, elegant appetizer. A touch of garlic and onion adds depth, while a swirl of cream can elevate it to a more luxurious texture.

This soup is also incredibly versatile. It pairs beautifully with grilled cheese sandwiches, crusty bread, or a simple green salad. Whether blended until silky or left slightly chunky, tomato basil soup is both easy to prepare and impressive to serve, making it a staple in kitchens around the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (400g) chopped tomatoes
  • 250 ml vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves (or 1 tsp dried)
  • 50 ml heavy cream (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Croutons or toasted bread, to serve

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, vegetable broth, and sugar. Stir well.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  6. Add the fresh basil and blend the soup using a stick blender until smooth (or leave slightly chunky, if preferred).
  7. Stir in the cream, if using, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Simmer for another 2–3 minutes, then serve hot with croutons or a slice of crusty bread.
